Welcome to my personal page! I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Boston University. My research spans the fields of political economy and development economics. My job market paper studies legitimacy concerns as a driver of electoral fraud in autocracies. My other projects include a study on gendered demand for transportation in urban Ethiopia, and social network analysis to estimate the effectiveness of strategic voting in solving the collective action problem. In my work, I use quasi-experimental methods and have experience designing and implementing randomized controlled trials.
