Welcome to my personal page! I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Boston University. My research spans the fields of political economy and development economics. My job market paper studies legitimacy concerns as a reason to conduct elections and commit electoral fraud in an autocracy. My other projects include work on gendered demand for transportation, strategic voting and solving the collective action problem. In my work, I use a quasi-experimental approach and have experience designing and implementing randomized controlled trials.

I will be giving talks in Fall 2024 at: IPSA 75th Anniversary Conference, Lisbon. Please reach out to me if you want to chat during one of these events!
